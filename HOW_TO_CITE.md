# How to cite sIATC

Citing is a courtesy, not a licence obligation (Apache 2.0 does not require
citation in published work). But if sIATC helped you produce or document an
output, a citation supports the open, reflective culture the tool is built for.

Two things should be cited together: **sIFAC**, and the **upstream sIfA tool**
it is derived from.

## Cite sIFAC

> Ferreira, L. (2026). *sIATC - Statement of Intellectual Accountability and Transparency for Content* (Version 1.2) [Computer software]. Zenodo. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20733182

## Cite the upstream sIfA tool (always include alongside)

> Schomerus, M. (2026). *sIfA Tool — Statement of Intellectual Fellowship and
> Accountability* (Version 1.2) [Computer software]. Busara.
> https://github.com/mareikeschomerus-ctrl/sIfA.
> https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20285993

## In-text mention (short form)

> The AI contribution statement for this content was produced using sIATC
> (`Luis Ferreira`, 2026), adapted from the sIfA Tool (Schomerus, 2026).

## Caption for the sIFAC figure

> Statement of Intellectual Accountability and Transparency for Content (sIATC) for this work. The orange field represents human contribution; the inner purple shape represents AI interaction; each axis is one content stage. Generated with sIATC v1.2, adapted from the sIfA Tool (Schomerus, 2026). Responsibility for the content rests with the human author(s).
